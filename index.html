Introduction to Athboy

Athboy from the Irish “Baile Átha Buí” meaning “Town of the Yellow Ford”, coming from the fact that Athboy is built on the Yellow Ford River, now more commonly called the Athboy River.

Athboy is a village in Co. Meath, steeped in history, more information about this on the history page. With a strong community spirit with activities and clubs being a major part of the local community. The population of Athboy is ~2,500. Athboy is located in the West of Meath, near the border of Meath & Westmeath. Close to the Towns of Trim, Navan & Kells in County Meath and only 65 kilometres from Dublin.

Athboy has its own festivals and events through the year, check them out on our events listings page.
